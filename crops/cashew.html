Cashew Cultivation Guide

1. Climate & Soil

  • Cashew thrives in warm, coastal, tropical climates with moderate rainfall.
  • Prefers sandy loam or red sandy soil with good drainage and pH 5.5–7.0.

2. Land Preparation & Planting

  • Clear land of weeds and debris; plow and level.
  • Plant grafted saplings in pits of 60x60x60 cm with compost.
  • Spacing: 7m x 7m (approx. 200 trees per acre).
  • Best planting season: June–August.

3. Irrigation

  • Water weekly for the first 2–3 years in dry areas.
  • Once established, minimal irrigation is needed except during flowering and nut development.

4. Fertilization

  • FYM (10 kg/tree) + NPK (500g N, 125g P, 125g K) per tree annually.
  • Split dose: half during June–July, remaining in Sept–Oct.

5. Pest & Disease Control

  • Pests: Tea mosquito bug, stem borer – use neem-based sprays or insecticides.
  • Diseases: Powdery mildew, anthracnose – apply fungicides and prune infected branches.

7. Yield & Returns

  • Yield starts from 3rd year; full yield after 7–8 years.
  • Expected yield: 5–8 quintals of raw cashew nuts per acre.

8. Market Rate & Profit

  • Market price: ₹120 – ₹180 per kg (raw nuts).
  • Gross returns: ₹60,000 – ₹1,40,000 per acre (mature orchards).
  • Net profit: ₹40,000 – ₹1,15,000 per acre.
