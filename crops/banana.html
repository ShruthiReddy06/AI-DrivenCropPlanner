Banana Cultivation Guide

1. Climate & Soil

  • Banana thrives in warm, humid climate with 20–35°C temperature.
  • Requires rich, well-drained loamy soil with pH 6–7.5.

2. Land Preparation & Planting

  • Plow and level land thoroughly; dig pits of 60x60x60 cm size.
  • Fill pits with FYM, neem cake, and topsoil before planting suckers or tissue culture plants.
  • Spacing: 6 ft x 6 ft (approx. 1200–1500 plants per acre).
  • Ideal planting season: June–July or Feb–March (depending on region).

3. Irrigation

  • Regular irrigation required every 7–10 days.
  • Drip irrigation is recommended for efficiency and better yield.

4. Fertilization

  • Apply FYM (10–15 kg/plant) and NPK (200:60:200 g/plant) in split doses.
  • Micronutrients like Zn, Mg, and B improve productivity.

5. Pest & Disease Control

  • Pests: Nematodes, banana weevil – apply neem cake and recommended pesticides.
  • Diseases: Panama wilt, Sigatoka – treat with fungicides and ensure good drainage.

7. Yield & Harvest

  • Harvest 11–12 months after planting.
  • Yield: 30–40 tons per acre depending on variety and practices.
