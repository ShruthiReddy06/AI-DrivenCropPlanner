Soyabean Cultivation Guide

1. Land Preparation

  • Prepare fine tilth by plowing 2–3 times and harrowing.
  • Ensure good drainage as soyabean is sensitive to waterlogging.
  • Apply 5–8 tons of FYM per acre before final plowing.

2. Seed Selection & Sowing

  • Use certified high-yielding and disease-resistant varieties.
  • Seed rate: 30–35 kg/acre.
  • Sow during June–July with 45 cm row and 5–7 cm plant spacing.
  • Seed treatment with fungicides and Rhizobium culture is recommended.

3. Water Management

  • Needs 3–4 irrigations depending on rainfall.
  • Critical stages: flowering, pod formation, and seed filling.
  • Avoid waterlogging and ensure proper drainage.

4. Fertilization Schedule

  • Basal application of DAP (50–60 kg/acre).
  • Micronutrients like zinc and molybdenum may enhance yields.
  • FYM helps improve soil structure and microbial activity.

5. Weed & Pest Management

  • Use pre-emergent herbicides or intercultural operations.
  • Common pests: girdle beetle, stem fly — treat with appropriate insecticides.
  • Diseases like rust and yellow mosaic — use resistant varieties and fungicides.

7. Harvesting & Yield

  • Harvest when leaves yellow and pods dry (90–110 days).
  • Expected yield: 10–15 quintals per acre.

8. Market Rate & Profit Estimation

  • Average market price: ₹4,000 – ₹6,000 per quintal.
  • Gross returns: ₹40,000 – ₹90,000 per acre.
  • Net profit: ₹30,000 – ₹75,000 per acre.
