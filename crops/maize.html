Maize (Corn) Cultivation Guide

1. Land Preparation

  • Plow the field 2–3 times to break up soil clods and improve aeration.
  • Level the field and ensure good drainage to prevent waterlogging.
  • Apply organic manure or compost to improve soil fertility.

2. Seed Selection & Sowing

  • Use certified, high-yield hybrid or composite maize seeds.
  • Sow during Kharif (June–July) or Rabi (October–November) seasons.
  • Spacing: 60–75 cm between rows and 20–25 cm between plants.
  • Sow seeds 3–5 cm deep for better germination.

3. Water Management

  • Germination Stage: Ensure adequate moisture until seedling establishment.
  • Knee-high & Flowering Stage: Critical stages needing proper irrigation.
  • Grain Filling: Maintain soil moisture to maximize yield.
  • Avoid Overwatering: Maize is sensitive to waterlogging.

4. Fertilization Schedule

  • Basal Dose: Urea (50 kg), DAP (25–30 kg), MOP (20–25 kg).
  • Top Dressing at Knee-high Stage: Urea (40–50 kg).
  • Silking Stage: Apply remaining Nitrogen and Potassium if needed.

5. Weed & Pest Management

  • Weeds: Pre-emergence herbicides like Atrazine; manual weeding at 20–30 days.
  • Pests: Fall Armyworm, Stem Borer — use approved insecticides promptly.
  • Diseases: Leaf Blight, Downy Mildew — use resistant varieties and fungicides.

7. Harvesting & Yield

  • Harvest when husks turn brown and kernels are hard and dry.
  • Expected yield: 25–40 quintals per acre with good practices.

8. Market Rate & Profit Estimation

  • Average market price: ₹1,300 – ₹2,000 per quintal.
  • Gross returns per acre: ₹32,500 – ₹80,000.
  • Net profit: ₹15,000 – ₹60,000 per acre.
