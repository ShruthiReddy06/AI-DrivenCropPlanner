Guava Cultivation Guide

1. Climate & Soil

  • Guava thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with dry winters.
  • Well-drained loamy soil with pH 5.5–7.0 is ideal.

2. Land Preparation & Planting

  • Plow and level the field, remove weeds and debris.
  • Dig pits of 60x60x60 cm and fill with FYM and topsoil.
  • Spacing: 5 m x 5 m or 6 m x 6 m (approx. 150–200 trees per acre).
  • Best planting time: July–September or February–March.

3. Irrigation

  • Water young plants weekly; mature trees need watering during dry periods.
  • Drip irrigation improves water use efficiency.

4. Fertilization

  • Apply FYM (20–25 kg/tree/year) and NPK (500:250:250 g/tree/year).
  • Micronutrient spray recommended for fruit quality.

5. Pest & Disease Management

  • Pests: Fruit fly, stem borer – use traps and sprays.
  • Diseases: Wilt, anthracnose – treat with fungicides and proper drainage.

7. Yield & Harvesting

  • Guava trees start yielding in 2–3 years; peak yield after 4–5 years.
  • Yield: 10–15 tons per acre annually.

8. Market & Profit

  • Average price: ₹10 – ₹25 per kg depending on season and variety.
